## Say hello to compute.fm

[compute.fm](https://www.compute.fm) is live. It's a licensed, always-on internet radio station for people who build, deep-focus music for shipping code. Put it on, get in the zone, done.

Here's the fun part: we didn't really build it. We asked for it.

## It got one-shotted

The whole thing started with a single Slack message: *"build me a low-management internet radio station."*

A [Factory.ai](https://factory.ai) Droid read that message, went off, and came back with a working station. Not a scaffold. Not a TODO list. An actual streaming radio site, player, live now-playing metadata, our branding, the works, in one shot.

We fully expected to lose a weekend wiring this up. Instead we spent it arguing about which genre to stream.

## Then we just talked to it

Everything after the one-shot was pure vibes over Slack:

*"Put the brandmark and the wordmark on the same line."* Done.
*"Make it default to dark mode."* Done.
*"The music isn't actually playing."* Found it, fixed it, explained why.

Each request came back as a real pull request we could glance at and merge. It felt less like using a tool and more like DMing a very fast coworker who never sleeps and never gets tired of your nitpicks.

## Our favorite moment

At one point the stream would play but stay dead silent. We typed five words, *"the music isn't actually playing,"* and walked away.

The Droid traced the bug itself (the muted state wasn't getting reset when you hit Tune In), fixed it, checked its own work in a browser, and reported back with the root cause. We never opened the editor. We just merged.

## Why we love this

We build compute primitives for a living, the sandboxes and infrastructure that AI agents actually run on. So watching an agent take a one-line ask in Slack and ship a real, deployed product is more than a neat party trick to us, it's the whole thesis. The hard part stops being the typing and starts being deciding what to build.

Also we now have a radio station, which rules.

So put on [compute.fm](https://www.compute.fm), ship something, and enjoy the station an AI mostly built out of a Slack thread.
